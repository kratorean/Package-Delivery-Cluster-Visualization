Logistics Delivery Cluster Visualization
Project Overview
This project was developed to visualize clustered delivery activity across all delivery points within and around the FCT, Abuja, using geocoded delivery-location data and interactive geospatial mapping techniques.
The system uses Folium-based map visualization to display delivery/pick-up-density clusters, improve operational visibility, and support exploratory analysis of delivery concentration patterns across different delivery and pick-up locations.
________________________________________
Business Problem
Operational delivery and pickup data was previously analyzed primarily through spreadsheet-based reporting, which made it difficult to identify geographic delivery concentration, pickup density patterns, and high-value operational zones across the Federal Capital Territory (FCT).
Traditional tabular reporting provided limited spatial visibility into:
•	where the highest pickup activity was occurring,
•	which locations generated the highest operational demand,
•	and where additional operational resources could potentially improve business performance and revenue generation.
This limited the ability to make faster, geographically informed operational and commercial decisions.
________________________________________
Project Objective
The objective of this project was to develop an interactive geospatial visualization tool capable of:
•	mapping delivery and pickup activity, 
•	identifying high-density operational area within the FCT, 
•	improving visibility into package movement patterns, 
•	supporting exploratory analysis of operational concentration, 
•	and providing insight into areas with strong package pickup activity that could support future operational and revenue-growth decisions.

Methodology
The workflow involved:
1.	Importing geocoded logistics datasets 
2.	Cleaning and validating latitude/longitude coordinates 
3.	Creating an interactive Folium base map 
4.	Applying clustered marker visualization techniques 
5.	Rendering delivery-density clusters using MarkerCluster 
Interactive map outputs were generated to visually represent operational delivery concentration across mapped regions.

Results & Operational Impact
The visualization provided immediate visibility into delivery and pickup concentration patterns that were not easily identifiable through conventional spreadsheet analysis.
The clustered geospatial view enabled:
•	faster identification of high-volume pickup and delivery locations, 
•	improved understanding of operational demand concentration, 
•	enhanced visibility into revenue-generating pickup areas, 
•	and more informed operational planning and resource-allocation decisions across the FCT. 
The project also established a foundation for future enhancements involving heatmaps, territory analysis, route optimization, and advanced logistics intelligence workflows.
